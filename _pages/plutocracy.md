A plutocracy (Greek: πλοῦτος, ploutos, 'wealth' + κράτος, kratos, 'power')
or plutarchy is a society that is ruled or controlled by people of great
wealth or income.

The first known use of the term in English dates
from 1631. Unlike systems such as democracy, capitalism, liberalism,
socialism or anarchism, plutocracy is not rooted in an established
political philosophy.
